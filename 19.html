Lesson 19

Today we're going to look at what happens when you have two consonants at the start of a syllable. When this happens, it's called a consonant cluster.

For the most part, you just... pronounce both letters. Take a guess - how do you think you read this word?

ฟรี frii free (an English word)

This word demonstrates two things that happen with consonant clusters:

  • The first consonant gives us the consonant class we use to figure out tone. This is a live syllable that starts with ฟ, which is low-class, which is why we have mid tone.
  • Things that go on top of or under consonants, like tone marks or vowels, go on top of the second consonant.

    • Now let's make it harder: give this one a try.

    โปรด bpròot empty
    Why is โปรด the tone that it is? It has a cluster that starts with ป, so we're going to use the mid consonant class to determine tone. Mid-class consonant plus dead syllable equals low tone.

    This one uses โอ! When things go to the left of a cluster, they go to the left of both consonants.

    Consonant clusters can make it hard to tell where syllables start and end. You might be tempted to read ฟรี as ฟ-รี fá-rii or โปรด as โป-รด bpoo-rót. It's also tricky when ว is a consonant in a cluster. It can sometimes look like the vowel อว. You just have to know the word - in practice, it's not so bad, I promise!

    Do all those rules look familiar, by the way? There's a lot of the same things happening with the ห rule and the อย words from lesson 15. For both of those clusters, it's still the first consonant that gives us the consonant class: adding ห makes a cluster high-class, and all four อย words are low tone because the อ makes the cluster mid-class. The same spelling rules apply, too - the only difference is that in those two special clusters, the ห and อ are silent.

    All we've really started doing, then, is pronouncing both consonants in a cluster, like you do in these words. Sometimes they might be hard to find, especially next to other consonants or when "wrapped" by vowels, but take your best guess. There's at least one cluster in each word. (There are also some funky ending consonants - remember the rules from last lesson!)

    Other rules

    Dead syllables end in one short vowel or p t k. All other syllables are live.

    Syllables with no written vowels have inherent vowels: a for one letter and o for two.

    The only permitted ending sounds are m n ng p t k. Consonants at the end of a syllable change to the closest-sounding ending sound:

    "Normal sound" Ending sound
    b bp p p
    d dt t j ch s t
    g k k
    r l n
    ญ but not ย n

    ห plus a sonorant or sing-y letter makes it high-class.

    Words starting with อย have low tone.

    When two consonants combine at the start of a syllable, nothing goes between them and they always stay together. Things that are written on top or below get written on the second consonant.
