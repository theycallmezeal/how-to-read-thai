"Phonetic Thai"

As far as I can tell, "phonetic Thai" is mostly a common-sense thing instead of any standardized set of rules. But if I had to say what most people probably do:

  • Dashes between syllables (no more wondering where syllables start and end!)
  • อะ instead of the inherent vowel a
  • The inherent vowel o isn't written - you know it's there when there's a vowelless syllable, since all other vowels are written
  • Lots of the ห rule - sometimes with a dot underneath the ห to indicate that it's not a regular letter h (see below)
  • Just the "nice" consonants, no Sanskrit / Pali consonants: ก ข ค ง จ ฉ ช ซ ด ต ถ ท น บ ป ผ ฝ พ ฟ ม ย ร ล ว ส ห อ ฮ
  • Only ม น ง บ ด ก at the ends of syllables
  • Vowels mostly as normal, but the various ways of writing ai all become ไอ
  • Tone marks as normal
  • อ็ is written on top of tone marks and vowels when you're officially not allowed to (see below)
