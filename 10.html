Lesson 10

Today's theme: vowels! I'm tired of having so few vowels to read. Let's give ourselves some more to play with.

We should start by recapping the vowels that we do know. Here they are. Read them, and make sure you take note of whether they're short or long. As we've seen, this is important for tone!

อุ u short vowel
อะ a short vowel
ไอ ai two short vowels
โอ oo long vowel
อา aa long vowel
อี ii long vowel
ใอ ai short vowels
อู uu long vowel
อั a short vowel
อิ i short vowel
Here's another recap: what are these letters? What classes do they belong to? อ ว อ is the silent consonant; it's middle-class. ว is w, and it's low-class.

Here's something really crazy. These two letters can also be used as vowels:

Letter Romanization Length
ออ ɔɔ long
อัว uua long vowel + short vowel
อว uua long vowel + short vowel

If you're a speaker of American English, take particular note of the difference between โอ oo and ออ ɔɔ. I personally find the difference kind of hard to hear.

อว and อัว are the same thing! อว is used when there's a consonant at the end of the syllable. อัว is used when there isn't.

Pop quiz: what other vowel is spelled two different ways, depending on whether there's a consonant at the end of a syllable? It's a! It's spelled อั when there's a consonant at the end of the syllable and อะ when there isn't.

The fact that these two letters, อ and ว, play double duty as both consonants and vowels, makes reading kind of tricky. Often, when looking at a new word, you won't know which they are - these two letters can make reading new words ambiguous. But you can often "vibe with it" and make a good guess, which I hope you'll discover as you try out these words:

ขอ kɔ̌ɔ may I have...?
ของ kɔ̌ɔng things or stuff
ขอบ kɔ̀ɔp to accept
คอ kɔɔ neck
บอก bɔ̀ɔk to tell
ออก ɔ̀ɔk east or out
ออม ɔɔm to save or conserve
ขวด kùuat bottle
วัว wuua beef
นวด nûuat massage
หัว hǔua head
อวด ùuat to gloat
ชวน chuuan to persuade or chá-wá-ná quickness / wit (yup, these two words are spelled the same!)

Check out the word นวด in particular. It's a dead syllable that starts with a low-class consonant. Were you able to figure out that อว uua counted as a long vowel, making the tone falling?

I'll introduce one more group of vowels. (There's only one more group after this!)

The last set of vowels all have this guy: เ that likes to hang out on the left of a consonant. Check these out:

Letter Romanization Length
เอ ee long
แอ ɛɛ long
เอา ao two short vowels
เอิ əə long
เออ əə long

Once again, we have a vowel that's spelled two different ways depending on whether the syllable ends in a consonant. Use เอิ with an ending consonant, and เออ without. That extra อ might look like a separate consonant sometimes, so be careful!

Let's try these out:

เวลา wee-laa time
เท tee to pour
เอก èek sole, solitary
โรงแรม roong-rɛɛm hotel
แก gɛɛ a very casual word for "you"
แยก yɛ̂ɛk intersection
แสง sɛ̌ɛng light (like "there's a lot of light in this room", not a physical lightbulb or something)
เทา tao gray
เทวดา tee-wá-daa god / deity (make sure not to confuse เทว tee-wá, which is two separate syllables, with เทา tao!)
เรา rao we
เอา ao to bring
เกินไป gəən-bpai too (as in "it's too late")
เลิก lə̂ək to lift something up
เกิด gə̀ət to take place
เถิด tə̀ət a particle that kind of means "let's"

Lovely! We've now met almost all of our "basic" vowel signs. In this lesson, we didn't do anything new with tone, but I hope you got some good practice with the tone rules with all those practice words. In our next lesson, we're going to start tackling one of the biggest and most important parts of Thai writing: the tone marks. Get ready!

Review

Consonants

High-class

ข ฉ ถ ผ ฝ ษ ส ห

Mid-class

ก จ ฎ ฏ ด ต บ ป อ

Low-class

ค ง ช ซ ท น พ ฟ ภ ม ย ร ล ว ฮ

Vowels

Long vowels

อา เอ แอ เอิ เออ อี ออ โอ อู

Short vowels

อั อะ อิ อุ

Diphthongs

ไอ ใอ อัว อว เอา

Tone rules

First consonant Tone
live dead
short long
low-class mid high falling
mid-class mid low
high-class rising low

Other rules

Dead syllables end in one short vowel or p t k. All other syllables are live.

Syllables with no written vowels have inherent vowels: a for one letter and o for two.

บ ค ก sound like p t k at the end of a syllable.
