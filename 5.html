Lesson 5

Let's meet two new letters.

Letter Romanization Class
ซ s low
ฮ h low
What kind of sound are they? What classes could these letters be in? They are fricatives or blowy sounds, so they could be low or high-class.

You'll have to remember that they are low-class consonants! These letters are mostly used in loanwords. Try reading these words. Some will sound familiar!

ซานา saa-naa Sana'a, the capital of Yemen
ซีตา sii-dtaa the Greek letter zeta
ซี sii the English letter C
ซูดาน suu-daan the country Sudan
โซน soon zone
โซดา soo-daa soda
ฮาม haan an archaic word meaning "dawn" (I had to look really hard to find words with ฮ, lol)
ไฮโซ hai-soo bougie (from "high society")
ฮาโลวีน haa-loo-wiin Halloween
Remember the letters ค ท? What class are they? ค is k and ท is t. They're both low-class.
What kind of sound do they make? What's the other consonant class that letters with these sounds could make? They're both aspirated stops or puffy sounds. Letters with these sounds could be either low-class or high-class.
What are the two kinds of sounds whose letters could be low-class or high-class? Aspirated stops / puffy sounds and fricatives / blowy sounds.

So far, all the aspirated stop and fricative letters (puffy and blowy sounds) we've learned have been low-class. However, each of these sounds will actually have two letters: one low-class and one high-class. So let's review all of these low-class letters and meet their high-class buddies:

Sound Low-class letter High-class letter
p พ ผ
t ท ถ
ch ช ฉ
k ค ข
s ซ ส
h ฮ ห

Some letters look really similar! Take note of these for now. I have all the consonants sorted out by shape in the appendix.

  • ข ช ซ บ ป
  • ด ต ค
  • พ ผ
  • อ ฮ

Note that only the aspirated stops and fricatives (puffy and blowy sounds) have both low-class and high-class letters. We've met lots of other low-class consonants that make other sounds, but they don't have high-class buddies.

So why does class matter? The answer: tone. For now (we'll be adding to this later), here's the rule:

  • Syllables starting with low or mid-class consonants have mid tone.
  • Syllables starting with high-class consonants have rising tone.

At last, we've broken free of the mid tone! We're now playing with two out of five total tones, and we've met consonants in all three consonant classes - low, mid, and high. This is a big day!

Note that the names of the consonant classes have no relation to the tone of the syllables they're in! As you've seen, low-class consonants can give us syllables with mid tone, for example.

How can you remember which letters are low or high-class? My suggestion: use the names of the letters. The names of the high-class letters are pronounced with rising tone; the others use mid tone. ซ is sɔɔ, but ส is sɔ̌ɔ.

Here are some words with our new high-class consonants. Remember to read their syllables with rising tone. Caution: there are some syllables that don't start with high-class consonants mixed in! Remember the rule.
