Lesson 4

We know three vowels already: one that attaches on the right of a consonant, one that attaches on top, and one that attaches on the bottom.

What are the three vowels? อา aa attaches to the right, อี ii attaches to the top, and อู uu attaches to the bottom.

Now I'm going to show you something crazy. There are vowels that attach to the left.

Letter Romanization Length
ไอ ai two short vowels
ใอ ai two short vowels
โอ oo long

Even though it looks like the vowel comes first, you pronounce it after the consonant it attaches to, just like the other vowels!

Practice them with these words:

ใน nai inside
ไป bpai to go
โรง roong building

ไอ and ใอ are diphthongs: multiple vowel sounds smooshed together as one letter. They both make the same sound, ai. That's a short a and short i. What's the difference between ไอ and ใอ? It's all spelling! Most words use ไอ. There are only 20 words that use ใอ, and you just have to remember them.

If you're an English speaker, it's easy to pronounce oo by closing your lips at the end, so it sounds more like an o plus a u. In Thai, it's one single o sound, held long without changing.
