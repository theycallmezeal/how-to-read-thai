Lesson 3

We're going to add a whopping nine letters to our arsenal today, starting with six consonants:

Letter Romanization Class
ด d mid
ต dt mid
ท t high
บ b mid
ป bp mid
พ p high

These sounds can be hard to hear the difference between.

ท and พ are our aspirated or puffy sounds. You should make sure to get a good puff of air when you say them.

What consonant classes could they be in and why? Because they're aspirated or puffy, they could be either high or low-class.

Both of them are low-class. Memorize that!

ด d and ต dt sound similar and look similar. For ด d, give it more "oomph" than you would an English "d". You should feel your vocal cords really vibrating for it. They shouldn't vibrate when you pronounce ต dt - there's no oomph at all for this sound. Then, remember the puff of air that comes with ท t.

It's the same deal for บ b and ป bp. Give บ b some oomph and put your vocal cords into it. Then make sure you don't put any oomph into ป bp - no vocal cord vibrations for this one. And lastly, พ p has a puff of air.

What consonant class are ด ต บ ป in? Why? They're mid-class consonants because they're all unaspirated stops or touchy sounds.

With our new letters, try reading these words, being careful about your pronunciation. They're all still mid tone:

ตา dtaa maternal grandfather
ตาม dtaam to follow
ทา taa to smear something on something
พา paa to lead someone somewhere

This long aa is getting boring. But to learn more vowels, we first need to learn this special consonant:

Letter Romanization Class
อ nothing! mid

It's a silent consonant! Its name is just ɔɔ. Every Thai vowel needs to be "attached" to something. So if the syllable starts with a vowel, we need to use อ as a placeholder for the vowel to attach to.

That means we shouldn't have been writing า on its own in lesson 1. How should we have written the syllable aa? อา

อา aa is a word, too! It means "uncle".

อ is a mid-class consonant! Just remember that.

Let's use อ to finally introduce some more vowels.

Letter Romanization Length
อี ii long
อู uu long

Check it out! These vowels don't go to the right of the consonant like อา does. They go on top and under the consonants they're "attached" to. See how using a consonant like อ shows us where they attach?

Try reading these words, again using all mid tone:

ดี dii good
ตี dtii to hit
ที tii a time, like "one time"
มี mii to have
ปี bpii year
บางที baang-tii sometimes
รู ruu hole
ดู duu to look at
ทูน tuun to revere or respect
งู nguu snake

Check it out, บางที is our longest word yet!

บางที has two syllables. What are they? บาง baang and ที tii.

How did you know where the syllables separated? Here, I bet you just knew: baang-tii just makes sense. It's not always that easy, but for now - pay attention to where syllables start and end!
